# Smaran — Ground-Contact Kit
### Ten conversations that decide the fork
*v1.0 · July 2026 · Companion to Blueprint v3.0 Part 9. This kit resolves promotion condition ① and gathers the field evidence for ② . Print the scorecard (§6) — one per interview.*

---

## §1 — What this decides, and the one discipline that matters

These interviews decide whether Smaran gets built. Pass → hand Parts 5–7 to the coding agent. Fail → shelve without a line of code. Both outcomes are wins.

**The discipline: never ask about the future, ask about the past.** "Would you pay?" produces courtesy, especially toward a respectful visitor, and doubly so in a culture of hospitality. "What happened last time?" produces truth. Every question below is built on this. The concept is NOT revealed until §4 Step 5 — the first two-thirds of each conversation is about *his practice*, not your product. If you catch yourself pitching before Step 5, stop; the interview is degrading into a demo.

**Who qualifies (screen before booking):** independent urban purohit — self-employed (not on a fixed temple salary), serves his own yajman families (not marketplace-assigned clients), city-based, active practice. Disqualify: salaried archakas, marketplace-only priests, retired. Aim for 10 qualified; expect to talk to 15 to get there.

**Logistics:** his turf (temple premises, his home) > yours. 30–45 minutes. Take dakshina etiquette seriously — a small respectful token on leaving, never framed as payment for the meeting. Ask permission before any notes; no recording unless volunteered. Language: whichever he leads with. Address as Purohit-ji / Panditji / Guruji per local convention.

**Recruiting the ten:** start from one warm introduction (family purohit, neighbour's, temple office) and ask each interviewee at the end for one introduction — this doubles as your first k-signal (§6, row K1). Two or three temple-dense wards of the intended seed city only.

---

## §2 — Opening (5 min): earn the right to ask

Introduce yourself as someone studying how purohits manage their practice — not as someone with an app. One line: *"Main samajhna chahta hoon ki aap apni yajman families ka hisaab aur tithiyan kaise sambhalte hain."* Then map the practice, easy questions first:

1. How many yajman families do you serve regularly? How long have the oldest been with you? *(hub size — feeds the 30-families assumption)*
2. How did most of them come to you — inherited from your father, referred, or new?
3. What does a normal week look like right now vs. Shravan/festival season?

---

## §3 — The pain excavation (15 min): stories, not opinions

### A. Memory & the bahi khata (ingestion evidence)
4. How do you keep track of every family's dates — the shraddh tithis, annual kathas? **Ask to see it.** The diary/notebook/register is the single most informative artifact of the whole interview: photograph it (with permission), note the fields he keeps (names? gotra? tithi format? amounts?).
5. Walk me through how you'd check, right now, whose rituals fall in the next two weeks. *(watch the actual process — minutes of page-flipping is the pain, live)*
6. **Micro-test:** "Would you mind if I take a photo of one page? I want to see if something I'm working on can read it." His reaction to photographing the khata previews the entire ingestion assumption.

### B. The missed date & the lapsed yajman (the Recover thesis)
7. Has there ever been a time a family's date was missed — by you or by them? What happened? *(let the story run; note emotional register)*
8. Think of families who used to call you every year and have gone quiet. How many, roughly? Why do you think they stopped?
9. What do you do today when a family goes quiet — do you reach out? Why / why not? *(the awkwardness of chasing is the product's opening)*
10. Have any families started using apps or online pujas instead? What do you think of those platforms? *(unprompted marketplace resentment = wedge confirmation; note his exact words)*

### C. Festival season & scheduling (the Protect thesis)
11. Tell me about last Shravan / Navratri — how did you manage the bookings? Ever had two families expect you in the same muhurat? What happened?

### D. Dakshina (the Collect thesis — tread gently)
12. These days do families give dakshina in cash or on the phone (UPI)? Has that changed anything?
13. Is there ever awkwardness about it — families who delay, or where you can't ask? *(do not push; one story is enough)*

### E. Digital habits
14. What do you use WhatsApp for in your practice today? *(forwarding festival messages? coordinating? — existing behaviour the product rides)*
15. Do you use any app or calendar for panchang? Which?

## §4 — The reveal & the three tests (15 min)

### Step 5 — Reveal (only now, and in one breath)
*"Ek WhatsApp seva hai — aap apni bahi khata ek baar bol kar ya photo se usme daal dete hain. Phir woh har parivar ki tithi aapko pehle se yaad dilati hai, aapke naam se parivar ko sundar card bhejti hai, jo yajman chhoot gaye hain unki yaad dilati hai, aur festival season mein double-booking se bachati hai. Paisa seedha aapke UPI mein — koi commission nahi, koi marketplace nahi, aapke yajman sirf aapke."*
Then silence. Let him respond first. Note his **first question** verbatim — first questions reveal the value rung he actually cares about (a reminder question = rung 1; "woh gaye hue yajman waali baat…" = rung 2, the money rung).

### Test 1 — Price (commitment, not opinion)
16. State it plainly: ₹1,101 per year — and for the first purohits of this area, the founding year is ₹501 (shagun amount, first 100 only). Then: **"Agar main agle mahine shuru karoon, kya aap pehle purohits mein hona chahenge? Main aaj aapki 3 family abhi likh loon?"**
Score by what he *does*, not says: offers to pay / gives families to load now / gives his number and says start = **commit**. "Achha hai, dekhenge" = courtesy. Price objection with a counter-number = engaged (note the number). Silence or subject-change = no.

### Test 2 — Family layer (permission, not speculation)
17. Do NOT ask "will families pay ₹21?" — he can't know. Ask instead: **"Kya aap comfortable honge agar aapke naam se, aapke parivaron ko unki apni tithiyon ka calendar mile — ₹21 mahina, aur woh hamesha aapke naam se hi jaaye?"** Purohit resistance here kills attach regardless of family demand; enthusiasm ("mere yajman ke liye achha hai") is the green light. Bonus: ask for introductions to 2–3 of his families for a five-minute version of the same conversation — direct attach evidence beats all speculation.

### Test 3 — The k question (sabha mapping)
18. Which purohit sabha / association / mandal are you part of? How often does it meet? Who runs it?
19. **"Aapke jaise aur kaun purohit hain jinko yeh kaam aayega?"** — count how many names come *unprompted*, and whether he offers an introduction without being asked. A spontaneous "main Tiwari-ji ko bataunga" is worth more than any stated intent.
20. If a sabha exists: would he introduce you to present there? *(one sabha presentation = the seeding model; a concrete yes here is the strongest k evidence a conversation can produce)*

Close: thanks, token of respect, one referral ask, and note whether he messages you afterward unprompted (post-interview pings are commitment-grade signal).

## §5 — Traps

- **The courtesy yes.** Warm words + no action = no. Only §6's observable behaviours count.
- **Pitching early.** Every minute of pitch before Step 5 contaminates every answer after it.
- **Leading the pain.** Never "isn't it hard to remember all the tithis?" — ask what happened, and let hard emerge or not.
- **The enthusiastic outlier.** One delighted purohit is an anecdote. The scorecard aggregates ten; decide on the aggregate.
- **Translating your excitement.** If an answer thrills you, ask one more neutral follow-up before writing it down.
- **Interviewing the wrong priest.** A marketplace-assigned or salaried priest will love the idea and never pay — the screen in §1 exists for this.

## §6 — Scorecard (one per interview) & decision rules

| # | Signal | Observable | Maps to |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Price commitment | Paid / gave families to load / "start today" with number | Conversion 30% |
| P2 | Engaged price counter | Objected with a specific counter-number (record it) | Pricing input |
| R1 | Lapse resonance | Named ≥2 lapsed families unprompted; emotional register on Q8–9 | Recover thesis |
| W1 | Wedge confirmation | Unprompted marketplace resentment (verbatim quote) | Positioning |
| I1 | Khata exists & photographable | Showed it; allowed photo; fields noted | Ingestion path |
| I2 | Voice/WhatsApp fluency | Uses WA actively in practice (Q14) | F3 assumption |
| F1 | Family-layer permission | Comfortable/enthusiastic on Q17; intros offered | Attach 5% |
| K1 | Spontaneous referral | Named fellow purohits unprompted; offered intro | k ≥ 1.3 |
| K2 | Sabha access | Named a sabha + concrete path to present there | Seeding model |
| C1 | Collect resonance | UPI already in use; awkwardness story on Q13 | Collect thesis |

**Decision rules (aggregate over 10 qualified interviews):**
- **PASS → build:** P1 ≥ 4 **and** R1 ≥ 6 **and** I1 ≥ 7 **and** F1 ≥ 5 **and** (K2 ≥ 2 concrete sabha paths **or** K1 ≥ 6).
- **FAIL → shelve:** P1 ≤ 2, **or** R1 ≤ 3 (the income-protection thesis itself is wrong), **or** I1 ≤ 4 (no ingestion path — khatas don't exist in usable form).
- **Between the lines → one written parameter revision** per Toolset rules: re-run the back-solve with what the field actually said (e.g., the modal P2 counter-price), attach it, and decide on the revised arithmetic. A second revision is a shelve.
- Record every scorecard; they enter the registry as the named test result either way. If pass, the P2 modal price and K1/K2 details become inputs to the pre-registration record before build.

---
*One person, one ward, roughly two weeks, near-zero cost. The cheapest decisive test in the entire portfolio — which is exactly why it runs before any code.*
