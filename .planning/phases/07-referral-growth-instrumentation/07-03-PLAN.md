## Design note — what observed k actually is
Viral coefficient k = (activated users generated via referral) / (users who could refer). To clear
k >= 1.3, each referrer must activate, on average, >1 downstream purohit — a ratio that MUST be able
to exceed 1. The shipped formula divides referred signups by total same-week signups, which is a
proportion <= 1 by construction; it answers a different question and can never express the gate.
The correct computation attributes each activated referred purohit back to its REFERRER's cohort
(the week the referrer signed up), then per cohort: k = (# activated purohits whose
referred_by_purohit_id points at a member of this cohort) / (# activated purohits in this cohort).
"Activated" = the project's activation predicate (at minimum: completed onboarding; tighten to
"logged >=1 event" if that is the agreed activation bar — confirm at Task 1). This is unbounded above
and is the number the M4 gate reads. Keep the internal-key gating and the OpenAPI/zod contract; only
the SQL and the response semantics change (add referrer-cohort fields; observedK may exceed 1).
