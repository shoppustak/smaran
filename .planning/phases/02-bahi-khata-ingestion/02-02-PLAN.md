Exact canonical tables from `docs/ref-state-2-plan.md` §5.2 (transcribe verbatim, Devanagari accuracy
is load-bearing — do not re-derive or paraphrase):

Maas (12) + variants: Chaitra(चैत्र, चैत, Chait) · Vaishakha(वैशाख, बैसाख, Baisakh) ·
Jyeshtha(ज्येष्ठ, जेठ, Jeth) · Ashadha(आषाढ़, असाढ़, Asadh) · Shravana(श्रावण, सावन, Sawan) ·
Bhadrapada(भाद्रपद, भादो, भादों, Bhado) · Ashwina(आश्विन, क्वार, कुँवार, Kwar) ·
Kartika(कार्तिक, कातिक, Katik) · Margashirsha(मार्गशीर्ष, अगहन, Aghan) · Pausha(पौष, पूस, Poos) ·
Magha(माघ, Magh) · Phalguna(फाल्गुन, फागुन, Phagun)

Tithi (map to SMALLINT 1-15) + variants: Pratipada/पड़वा(1), Dwitiya/दूज(2), Tritiya/तीज(3),
Chaturthi/चौथ(4), Panchami(5), Shashthi/छठ(6), Saptami(7), Ashtami(8), Navami(9), Dashami(10),
Ekadashi/ग्यारस(11), Dwadashi/बारस(12), Trayodashi/तेरस(13), Chaturdashi/चौदस(14),
Purnima/पूनम = tithi 15 + paksha Shukla, Amavasya/अमावस = tithi 15 + paksha Krishna (dual-field
entries — spoken Purnima/Amavasya sets BOTH the tithi number and implies paksha).

Paksha (2): Shukla(शुक्ल, सुदी, Sudi) · Krishna(कृष्ण, बदी, Badi, वदी) — Sudi/Badi are extremely
common in speech and must be present.

Gotra (~150-entry semi-open seed list, no-match is valid): Kashyap, Bharadwaj, Vashishtha,
Vishwamitra, Atri, Gautam, Jamadagni, Agastya, Shandilya, Garg, Kaushik, Parashar, Vatsa,
Angirasa, ... (comment in the file that this is a semi-open seed list, not exhaustive).

Fuzzy-match rules (§5.3, exact, non-negotiable):
Normalize (NFC, strip diacritic noise, lowercase Latin) -> exact variant-table hit -> else
Levenshtein against canonical+variants, per-field thresholds: maas/tithi/paksha accept <=2
edits (closed sets, aggressive); gotra <=1 edit (protective); family_name never auto-corrected
(skip fuzzy-matching entirely for family_name).
